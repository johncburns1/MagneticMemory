Magnetic Drum




A look at Andrew Booth's original blueprints of his magnetic drum memory for computers (1948)

  • Invented by Austrian engineer Gustav Tauschek in 1932
  • Metal cylinder, coated with ferromagnetic recording material
    • Stationary write heads emit an electrical pulse
    • The pulse changes the magnetic orientation of a particle at a given position on the drum
    • Read heads identify a particle's orientation as a 1 or a 0
    • Rotates
  • Andrew Donal Booth makes first magnetic drum 1946
    • English electrical engineer
    • Identified the major problem in computing to be a lack of storage
    • Was a mathematician as well and needed a reliable computer storage system to crunch numbers
  • Atlas 1 code-breaking computer (first stored program computer in the U.S.) --> UNIVAC 1101
  • Displaced by magnetic cores
  • Heavily influenced magnetic disk memory technology
